## SECTION 1: CONTENT STRATEGY

### Platform Prioritization (Ranked by ROI for Local B2B)

1. **LinkedIn** — B2B, decision makers, high purchase intent. Business owners actively looking to solve problems. Best for lead generation and authority building.
2. **Facebook** — Local business owner communities, Facebook groups, targeting by geography. Strong for community engagement and local reach.
3. **Instagram** — Visual portfolio, before/after galleries, personal brand, reels. Strong secondary platform for brand awareness and social proof.
4. **TikTok/Reels** — Viral potential, behind-the-scenes content, trending audio. Lower intent but high reach for brand awareness.

### Content Pillars (4 types, rotating)

**Pillar 1: Value Posts** — Educational content positioning you as the expert
- Why local businesses need a website (Google visibility, customer trust, 24/7 storefront)
- Common myths about websites (too expensive, takes too long, requires technical knowledge)
- Stats and data (% of businesses without websites, mobile search trends, Google visibility impact)
- SEO tips, Google Business Profile optimization, review generation strategies

**Pillar 2: Social Proof** — Showcase results and builds credibility
- Before/after website galleries (client wins)
- Client testimonials and case studies (with permission)
- Demo site showcases by niche ("Here's what a plumbing website looks like in 24 hours")
- Results metrics ("This HVAC company got 12 calls in week 1")

**Pillar 3: Behind the Scenes** — Humanize and show your process
- Time-lapse: "Building a website in 20 minutes"
- Day in the life content
- Your origin story ("Why I started this agency")
- How AI accelerates your work (without being too technical)

**Pillar 4: Direct CTA** — Conversion-focused, call-to-action heavy
- "DM me your business name, I'll build a free preview"
- Limited-time offers or promotions
- Direct appointment booking
- Free consultation offers

### Content Mix & Posting Frequency

**LinkedIn: 3x/week**
- Monday: Value post (tips, myths, stats)
- Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes or social proof
- Friday: Direct CTA (DM offer, booking link)

**Facebook: 3x/week (same content, adapted for community tone)**
- Monday: Value post (local business focus)
- Wednesday: Social proof + community building
- Friday: Direct CTA (group invite or DM offer)

**Instagram: 2x/week + 3-4 stories/week**
- Tuesday: Behind-the-scenes (Reels format)
- Thursday: Social proof (carousel or static post)
- Daily stories: Personal brand, quick tips, "day in the life"

**TikTok/Reels: 1x/week (minimum)**
- Friday or Saturday: High-energy, trending audio, educational or entertainment hook
- Repurpose best Instagram Reels here

## SECTION 7: OPTIMIZATION & ITERATION

### Track What Works (Monthly Review)

**Metrics to Watch:**

1. **LinkedIn:** Impressions, engagement rate, profile visits, connection requests
2. **Facebook:** Post reach, engagement, shares, DM inquiries
3. **Instagram:** Likes, comments, saves, shares, website clicks
4. **TikTok/Reels:** Views, shares, comments, profile visits

**Monthly Check-in (1 hour):**
- Pull analytics for each platform
- Identify top 3 posts by engagement
- Identify top 3 posts by DM/inquiry generation
- Notice patterns: what content type, topic, time, visual style gets best results
- Double down on what works
- Eliminate what doesn't

### Content Adjustments

**If a post gets high engagement but low leads:**
- The content is interesting but not converting
- Fix: Add stronger CTA, link to booking page, be more specific about offer

**If a post gets low engagement:**
- Wrong audience, wrong time, wrong topic, or weak headline
- Fix: Better headline, post at different time, adjust platform, make it more visual

**If TikTok outperforms Instagram:**
- Double down on TikTok
- Focus on trending audio, entertainment first, then education
- Might not be LinkedIn audience on TikTok—adjust copy
