## Key Findings for Product Development

### Top Features to Build (ranked by research impact):
1. Active recall card queue — 50-200% more effective than re-reading (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006)
2. 3-bucket spaced repetition system (Unknown → Learning → Known)
3. Interleaved question engine — 43% better retention (Taylor & Rohrer, 2010)
4. Confidence rating + calibration dashboard
5. Pre-session brain dump screen
6. Anxiety reappraisal prompt — 22% performance improvement (Brooks, 2014)
7. Sleep/study schedule optimizer

### Emergency Cram Protocol (Science-Backed):
- Minutes 0-20: Brain dump everything you know
- Minutes 20-40: Gap identification vs master list
- Minutes 40-100: Active recall on gaps (cover answer, attempt, check)
- Minutes 100-130: Mixed practice test (interleaved)
- Minutes 130-150: STOP. No new material. Let it consolidate.

### Counterintuitive Findings:
1. Re-reading is nearly useless (rated "low utility")
2. Confusion during interleaving = it's working
3. "Get excited" beats "calm down" before tests
4. Stop studying 30+ min before sleep
5. Students with highest confidence are most miscalibrated
6. 90-min nap ≈ full night sleep for memory consolidation
7. Weak areas first has higher ROI than reinforcing strengths
8. Brain dump before test = scientifically proven technique
9. Spacing > massing even with equal total hours
10. Harder retrieval = stronger memory (desirable difficulty)
