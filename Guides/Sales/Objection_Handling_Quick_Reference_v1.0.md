# Objection Handling Quick Reference

**Print this. Keep it on screen. Use during calls.**

| **OBJECTION** | **BEST RESPONSE** | **KEY PHRASE** |
|---|---|---|
| "It's too expensive" | Value reframe: 5 customers = revenue exceeds cost in month one | "One customer pays for the whole website" |
| "I need to think about it" | Dig deeper: "What specifically do you need to think through?" | Ask the real objection, then solve it |
| "Need to talk to my partner/spouse" | Loop them in now OR follow up with both | "Want to get them on the call right now?" |
| "Already have someone working on it" | Timeline check: "How long have they been saying that?" | "If it's not done in 2 weeks, you have us as backup" |
| "I don't need a website" | Google invisibility reframe | "Customers can't find you if you're not on Google" |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | Ask what to adjust, don't cut quality | "What matters more—saving $200 or making it work?" |
| "Bad experience with a web company" | Listen, then show your difference (no contracts, fixed price) | "We're different—you own it, no lock-in" |
| "Business is slow right now" | That's why NOW is the time to fix it | "Slow business = people aren't finding you" |
| "I'll do it myself eventually" | Time-cost calculation | "What's your hourly rate? We're cheaper than your DIY time" |
| "Send me more info" | Find out what specifically they need | "What exactly should I send? Design? Google process? Revisions?" |

## QUICK PLAYBOOK

**IF THEY HESITATE:** Ask, don't assume.

**IF THEY OBJECT:** Reframe, don't defend.

**IF THEY STALL:** Move to either/or close or urgency close.

**IF THEY SAY NO:** Stay in nurture, follow up in 30 days.

---

## CLOSING LINES (USE IN ORDER)

1. Assumptive: "So shall we get this live this week?"
2. Either/Or: "Would you prefer Quick Start or Growth?"
3. Urgency: "I can hold your spot for 48 hours."
4. Question: "What would you need to move forward?"
5. Take-Away: "Sounds like now's not the time. I'll check back in a month."
