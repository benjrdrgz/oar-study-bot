# Sales Call Script — AI Local Website Agency

## PRE-CALL PREP (30 seconds)

Before picking up the phone:

- **Check their demo site is live** — Verify the website we sent actually loads (it should be up within minutes)
- **Pull their Google reviews** — Search "[Business Name] + reviews" and note 1-2 specific positive reviews or patterns you can mention ("I see you've got great feedback on your customer service")
- **Note their niche and city** — Have it written in front of you (e.g., "Plumbing, Milwaukee, WI")
- **Quick business scan** — Look at their current website (if they have one) or Google Business profile; note 1 thing they're doing well
- **Set your mindset** — You're the expert here. They NEED this. You're here to help them, not sell them.

---

## OPENING (first 60 seconds)

**Script:**

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Agency Name]. Quick question — did you get a chance to see that demo site I sent over?"

**If YES:** "Perfect. What did you think?"
**If NO:** "No worries at all. It's a fully finished website that would be ready to go live for you this week. I sent it over so you could see exactly what we deliver. You can check it out in a second if you want, but I'm calling because I think there might be a real opportunity here for you to get online and start getting customers from Google. Do you have 5 minutes?"

**Goal:** Get them to engage, confirm they've seen the demo or are willing to listen.

---

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (3-5 minutes)

Ask these in natural conversation order. Listen more than you talk. Take notes.

1. **"How do customers currently find you?"**
   - Listen for: word of mouth, Google, yellow pages, social media, reputation
   - This reveals if they're Google-dependent or completely offline

2. **"Are you getting calls from Google right now?"**
   - Offline business? They'll say no.
   - This is the pain point.

3. **"What would 5 extra customers a month be worth to you?"**
   - Don't accept "I don't know." Push gently: "Just ballpark—if one customer spent $150-$300 with you, what's that worth?"
   - Write down the number. You'll reference it later.

4. **"Have you looked into getting a website before? What happened?"**
   - Past experience: "We didn't have the budget" / "It was too expensive" / "We tried but it was complicated"
   - This tells you their objection in advance.

5. **"Who else is involved in this decision?"**
   - Partner? Spouse? Co-owner?
   - If yes: "Perfect. Would it make sense to have them on the call?"

---

## THE PITCH (2 minutes max)

**Bridge Statement (from their problem → your solution):**

"Okay, so here's the thing—you've got a great [service/product] and people love you when they find you. The problem is they're not finding you. Nobody's Googling [business type] and seeing you. Our job is to fix that. We build you a professional website that shows up on Google, handles customer inquiries, and gets you booked up faster than you can say yes."

**Walk Through What They Get:**

"Here's exactly what you get: A fully custom website, optimized for Google so people in [City] can actually find you. We handle all the technical stuff—your reviews, your photos, your hours, all on one page. Customers can see your work, leave reviews, and call or book directly. That's it."

**Turnaround Time (say this with confidence):**

"We go live in 24 to 48 hours. Not weeks. Not months. This week."

**Social Proof:**

"I've been helping a lot of [niche] businesses in [city] get online this month. One plumber we launched is already getting 2-3 calls a week from Google. It works."

---

## PRICING PRESENTATION

**The Anchor Strategy:**

Always lead with Tier 2 (the mid-tier). It anchors high and makes everything else feel reasonable.

**Tier 2 First:**
"The growth package is $1,200. Fully custom website, we handle everything, you're live in 48 hours, and it's yours to keep and manage forever. No monthly fees, no surprise costs."

**Then Tier 1 (the "quick start"):**
"We also have a quick-start package at $697. It's pre-built framework, slightly faster deployment, perfect if you want to test the waters without the full investment."

**Tier 3 (only if they ask for more):**
"If you want the premium experience—unlimited revisions, video integration, custom photography—that's $2,400. But most people are happy with the growth package."

**Presentation Sequence:**
1. Lead with Tier 2 price
2. Explain what they get
3. If they hesitate, drop to Tier 1
4. Never go backwards from there (don't offer discounts on Tier 1)
5. If they want more, present Tier 3

## CLOSING TECHNIQUES

Use these in order of comfort level. Pick ONE per call.

### **The Assumptive Close (strongest)**
"So shall we get this live this week? Thursday or Friday?"
- Assumes they're buying
- Gives time options (not yes/no)
- 60% of the time, they'll just pick a day

### **The Either/Or Close**
"Would you prefer the quick-start package or the growth package?"
- Forces them to choose which one, not whether
- Removes the "yes/no" barrier
- Great if they're leaning in

### **The Urgency Close**
"Here's the thing—I can hold your site for 48 hours before I offer the slot to another business. That keeps you in priority position. Does this week work?"
- Creates mild scarcity (true, by the way)
- Doesn't feel pushy
- Focuses on timeline, not pressure

### **The Question Close**
"What would you need to see to move forward today?"
- Gets them to say the thing stopping them
- Shows you're listening
- Removes guessing

### **The Take-Away Close (use if they're stalling)**
"Sounds like now might not be the right time. Let me follow up with you in a month, okay? We'll be launching more sites by then, but I'll keep you on the radar."
- Removes pressure
- Sometimes rejection wakes them up
- Honest and respectful

## OBJECTION HANDLING

Below are the 10 most common objections. Use these responses word-for-word.

### **Objection 1: "It's too expensive"**

**Response:**
"I get that. Here's the way I think about it though—you said 5 extra customers a month would be worth [insert their number]. One customer pays for this entire website. So really, we're looking at the first customer covers it, and the next 4 are pure profit. Does that math make sense?"

**If they push back:**
"What if we did the quick-start at $697? Same site, slightly faster timeline, you're testing with less investment."

---

### **Objection 2: "I need to think about it"**

**Response:**
"Smart. What specifically do you want to think through? Is it the price, or do you have questions about what's included?"

**Listen.** They'll tell you the real objection. Address THAT, not the "think about it."

---

### **Objection 3: "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"**

**Response:**
"Perfect. That makes sense. Do you want to loop them in right now? I can quickly show both of you how this works. Takes 3 minutes."

**If they say no:**
"Okay. Send me their number or email and I'll follow up with both of you tomorrow with all the details. That way you're both on the same page."

---

### **Objection 4: "I already have someone working on it"**

**Response:**
"How long have they been saying it's coming?"

**If 1-3 months:**
"Okay, cool. If it's done in the next week or two, great. If not, you know how to reach me."

**If 3+ months:**
"Yeah, that's what I hear a lot. The problem is web people get busy and timelines slip. We specialize in doing this FAST. If your person doesn't deliver in the next two weeks, at least you have a backup plan. Deal?"

---

### **Objection 5: "I don't need a website"**

**Response:**
"I hear that. Here's the thing though—your customers are invisible to Google right now. Someone in [City] looking for [your service] doesn't see you. You lose that customer to your competition. A website isn't really about the website—it's about getting found. Does that make sense?"

**If they push back:**
"How many new customers do you get from word-of-mouth vs. people searching?"

---

### **Objection 6: "Can you do it cheaper?"**

**Response:**
"We could, but it wouldn't be the same experience. Here's what we can adjust: We can remove custom photography and use stock images instead—that saves you $200. Or we can simplify the design slightly—saves another $150. But I won't cut quality because your website needs to convert customers, not just exist. What matters more—saving money, or making sure this actually works?"

---

### **Objection 7: "I had a bad experience with a web company"**

**Response:**
"I'm sorry to hear that. That's actually pretty common. What happened?"

**Listen.** Then:

"Yeah, that's not how we operate. Here's our difference: You get a fixed price, a fixed timeline, and you're not locked into anything. If you don't like it, you own the site—take it and go. No contracts, no surprise fees. We make money when you're happy, and you're only happy if it actually works. Is that different from what you've experienced?"

---

### **Objection 8: "Business is slow right now"**

**Response:**
"That's exactly WHY you should do this. Right now is when you fix it. If business is slow, it's because people aren't finding you. This website is your fix. And it costs less than your monthly ad spend. When business picks back up, you'll be positioned to capture it."

---

### **Objection 9: "I'll do it myself eventually"**

**Response:**
"I respect that. Here's the math though: You could spend 20-30 hours learning web design, dealing with hosting, trying to rank on Google—or you could have it done and live this week and spend that time on your actual business. What's your hourly rate? Because if you're making more than $50-100 an hour doing what you do, it doesn't make financial sense to DIY this."

---

### **Objection 10: "Send me more info"**

**Response:**
"I will. But real quick—what specifically do you want to know more about? The design? How it works on Google? The revisions process? Because I want to send you exactly what's going to help you make a decision."

**If they can't articulate it:**
"Sounds like you might not be ready to move forward right now. I'll send over a quick one-pager and touch base in a week. Does that work?"

## POST-CALL ACTIONS

### **If they CLOSED (said yes):**
1. Send invoice + welcome packet within 1 hour
2. Schedule "kickoff call" for next day to gather info
3. Get testimonial scheduled for after launch

### **If they're THINKING (need to decide):**
1. Send follow-up email within 1 hour (see Follow-Up Template)
2. Schedule specific callback in 48 hours (not "I'll call you")
3. Add to nurture sequence (weekly touchpoint for 2 weeks)
4. Ask for email permission to send them case studies/proof

### **If they SAID NO:**
1. Ask if you can check in in 30 days
2. Add to long-term nurture (monthly touchpoint)
3. Document reason in CRM (too expensive / needs to think / bad past experience / etc.)
4. Don't disappear—stay in their head
