# Google Business Profile Setup Guide v1.0

v1.0 — Step-by-step GBP setup and optimization guide for client businesses

This guide is used as part of the Growth and Dominate tier deliverables. Covers full GBP setup, optimization, and ongoing management.

## 1. CLAIMING OR CREATING A GBP LISTING

1. Go to **business.google.com**
2. Click **Create account** (or sign in if existing)
3. Search for the business by name and address
4. If found, click "Claim this business"
5. If not found, click **"Create a new business on Google"** and fill in:
   - Business name (exact legal name, no keywords)
   - Service area (city/cities served)
   - Business address (full address, even if home-based)
   - Business category (primary)
   - Website URL
   - Phone number

## 2. VERIFICATION METHODS

Google will verify ownership via one of three methods:

- **Postcard verification** (5–7 days): Google mails a postcard with a code. Enter code in GBP to verify.
- **Phone verification** (immediate): Google calls the business number. Answer and follow prompts.
- **Email verification** (immediate): If business email is linked to Google account.

**Fastest:** Phone verification  
**Recommended:** Postcard (most reliable, less chance of missed calls)

## 3. OPTIMIZING EVERY FIELD

### Business Name
- Use exact legal business name
- No keywords, emojis, or symbols (Google penalizes this)
- Example: "Smith's Plumbing" ✓ | "Smith's Plumbing & Drain Cleaning Services" ✗

### Category (Primary + Secondary)
- Choose primary category that best describes the business
- Add 2–3 secondary categories for broader visibility
- Example for dentist: Primary = "Dentist" | Secondary = "Cosmetic Dentist," "Orthodontist"

### Description (750 characters)
- Opening sentence: What the business does, where, and why it's different
- Include service area (city names help local SEO)
- Mention specialties, years in business, unique value prop
- Natural keyword inclusion (not forced)

**Example:**
"Smith's Dental is a family dentistry practice in Portland and Beaverton, serving families for 20+ years. We specialize in cosmetic dentistry, implants, and pediatric care. New patients welcome."

### Hours
- List regular hours (Monday–Sunday)
- Add special hours for holidays (close dates, holiday hours)
- Update seasonally if applicable

### Photos (At Least 10; Google recommends 20+)
Guidelines for each type:

| Photo Type | Example | Best Practices |
|---|---|---|
| Logo | Business logo on white background | High resolution, square format |
| Cover | Storefront or main office entrance | Professional, well-lit, inviting |
| Interior | Main office/service area | Clean, organized, professional |
| Exterior | Full building/storefront view | Clear signage visible |
| Team | Staff photo or owner | Professional attire, smiling, welcoming |
| Products/Services | Product line or service in action | High quality, relevant, diverse |

**Photo best practices:**
- Minimum 720px width
- Recent photos (not old/dated)
- No watermarks or logos overlay
- Variety of angles and settings
- Rotate new photos monthly

### Services Section
Add all services offered:
- Plumbing: "Drain Cleaning," "Leak Repair," "New Installation," "Emergency Service"
- Dental: "Cleanings," "Fillings," "Root Canals," "Cosmetic Dentistry"
- Salon: "Hair Cut," "Hair Color," "Extensions," "Keratin Treatment"

For each service, optionally add:
- Service name
- Brief description (1–2 sentences)
- Price (optional)

### Products Section
*(If applicable; e.g., retail, restaurant, salon product lines)*

- Product name
- Description
- Price
- Photo

### Q&A Section (Pre-Populate)
Start a Q&A section with common questions:

**Q: What are your hours?**  
A: We're open Monday–Friday 9am–5pm, Saturday 10am–4pm, closed Sundays.

**Q: Do you offer emergency service?**  
A: Yes, we offer 24/7 emergency plumbing. Call [PHONE] after hours.

**Q: Do you offer online booking?**  
A: Yes, book appointments on our website at [WEBSITE].

This prevents low-quality Q&A and builds confidence.

## 4. REVIEW STRATEGY

Reviews are the #1 local ranking factor. Build a 30-day ramp-up plan:

### Email Template (To Customers)

Subject: "Help us grow—leave a review on Google"

Body:
---

Hi [CLIENT_NAME],

We love working with you and want to help more people discover us. If you had a great experience, would you mind leaving a review on Google? It takes 2 minutes and helps us more than you know.

Just click here: [GBP_REVIEW_LINK]

Thank you!

[BUSINESS_NAME]  
[PHONE]

---

### SMS Template (Post-Service)

---

Thanks for choosing [BUSINESS_NAME]! If you had a great experience, we'd love a Google review—it only takes 2 minutes. [GBP_REVIEW_LINK]

---

### Review Generation Playbook

**Week 1:** Send email to top 20 past happy clients  
**Week 2:** Send SMS to recent service completions  
**Week 3:** Follow-up email to non-responders  
**Week 4:** Incentivize reviews (contest: "5 reviews this week = free service month")

**Target:** 10–15 reviews in first 30 days

## 5. GBP POSTS STRATEGY

Post 1–2 times per week. Posts stay visible for 7 days, then archive.

### Post Types & Templates

**Service Spotlight Post**
"🔧 This week we're highlighting our [SERVICE]. Did you know [BENEFIT/STAT]? Get a free quote—link in bio. [CTA BUTTON: BOOK NOW]"

**Seasonal/Holiday Post**
"🎄 Happy Holidays! We're open [HOURS] this season. Call [PHONE] or book online—[LINK]."

**Team/Behind-the-Scenes**
"Meet the team! Our [EMPLOYEE_NAME] has been [ROLE] for [X] years. What's one thing you'd ask them? Drop a comment!"

**Promo/Limited Offer**
"⏰ This month only: [OFFER]. Valid through [DATE]. Appointments filling up—book now: [LINK]"

**Educational/Tips**
"Pro tip: [HELPFUL_TIP]. Don't let [PROBLEM] sneak up on you. Schedule your [SERVICE] today: [LINK]"

### Post Creation Schedule
- Monday: Service spotlight or tip
- Thursday: Promo, team feature, or seasonal

### Posting Best Practices
- Use 1–2 relevant emojis (not overly cutesy)
- 2–3 sentences max
- Always include a CTA button (CALL, MESSAGE, BOOK, LEARN_MORE, SHOP, SIGN_UP)
- Use business photos, not stock images
- Ask questions to drive engagement ("Which service are you considering?")

## 6. INSIGHTS: WHAT TO TRACK & REPORT MONTHLY

Pull these metrics from GBP **Insights** tab and report to client:

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Views | How many people saw your GBP listing |
| Clicks to Website | Traffic sent to website from GBP |
| Directions Requests | How many requested directions |
| Call Clicks | How many clicked to call |
| Customer Actions | Total engagement (views + clicks + calls) |
| Photo Views | Which photos get most attention |
| Search Terms | What clients are searching for |
| Review Rating | Current star rating (track trend) |
| Review Count | Number of reviews (target: +3–5/month) |

**Sample Monthly Report to Client:**
---

**Google Business Profile Performance — March 2026**

**Key Metrics:**
- Views: 240 (↑15% vs Feb)
- Calls: 18 (from GBP)
- Website Clicks: 12
- Reviews: 3 new (4.8★ average)

**Top Performing Photos:** Storefront, team photo  
**Top Search Terms:** "[Business] near me," "[Service] [City]"

**Recommendation:** Post 2x/week about [SERVICE] (getting top search traffic). Add 3 more interior photos to boost engagement.

## 7. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Keyword-stuffed business name | Google suppresses visibility, looks spammy | Use exact legal name only |
| Inconsistent address/phone | Confuses Google, hurts local rankings | Match website, directories, and signage exactly |
| No photos or low-quality photos | Lower engagement, less trustworthy | Use 15+ professional photos, update monthly |
| Duplicate GBP listings | Splits reviews and rankings | Merge duplicates or delete old listings |
| No posts for months | Algorithm thinks business is inactive | Post 1–2x per week minimum |
| Not responding to reviews | Looks unprofessional, hurts SEO | Respond to all reviews within 48 hours |
| Incorrect business hours | Wastes customer time, frustrates | Update for holidays and seasonal changes |
| Missing service area | Limits geographic visibility | Clearly define cities/regions served |
| Q&A section ignored | Let negative/false questions sit | Seed Q&A with quality questions answered by business |
| No review strategy | Reviews are the #1 local ranking factor | Set up email/SMS templates, weekly ask for reviews |
