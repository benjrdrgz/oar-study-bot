Newton's Three Laws of Motion — Visual Reference

Newton's Three Laws

1st — INERTIA
Object at rest stays at rest. Object moving keeps moving. Unless a force acts on it.
2nd — F = ma
Force = Mass × Acceleration
More mass = more force needed.
3rd — ACTION/REACTION
Equal & opposite on DIFFERENT objects. They do NOT cancel.

Force Formula

F = m × a
Force (N) = Mass (kg) × Acceleration (m/s²)
5 kg, 20 N force → a = 20/5 = 4 m/s²

Weight & Gravity

W = m × g
g = 9.8 m/s² (metric) or 32 ft/s² (imperial)
Free fall: v = gt  |  d = ½gt²

Golden Rule of Machines

Force × Distance = CONSTANT
Less force = more distance. You ALWAYS pay. No free lunch.

Pressure

P = F / A
Pressure = Force / Area
Smaller area = MORE pressure (stiletto vs flat shoe)

Pascal's Law (Hydraulics)

F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂
Small piston + small force → Big piston + BIG force

Continuity (Flow)

A₁v₁ = A₂v₂
Narrow pipe = faster flow

Bernoulli's Principle

Faster fluid = LOWER pressure
Explains airplane lift: air faster over curved top → lower pressure above → net upward force

Buoyancy (Archimedes)

Series vs Parallel Circuits

SERIES

— R₁ — R₂ — R₃ —
R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
✅ Current = SAME everywhere
⚡ Voltage = SPLITS
💀 One break = ALL die
(Christmas lights)

PARALLEL

┬ R₁ ┬
├ R₂ ├
┴ R₃ ┴
1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃
✅ Voltage = SAME everywhere
⚡ Current = SPLITS
✅ One break = others fine
(House outlets)

🧠 S = Same current, Sum resistance  |  P = same Pressure (voltage), splits current

READING COMPREHENSION

20 Qs • 30 min • 90 sec each

This is your EASIEST section — bank points here

You don't need to study content. You need to follow one rule and use the elimination strategy.

🚨 THE ONE RULE — MEMORIZE THIS

If the passage didn't say it,
the answer is WRONG.
Even if you know it's true. Even if it's obvious. If it's not in the text = wrong answer.

Strategy

1️⃣ Read first & last sentences for main idea
2️⃣ Skim body for structure
3️⃣ For detail Qs → find keywords in passage
4️⃣ Answer must be supported by text

Wrong Answer Red Flags 🚩

❌ Too extreme — "always," "never," "all"
❌ True but not stated — in the passage
❌ Opposite — of what passage says
❌ Too narrow — only covers one detail

Question Types & How to Attack

Main Idea
Read first/last sentences. Pick the answer that covers the WHOLE passage, not just one part.
Detail/Fact
Use keywords from the question to LOCATE the sentence. Answer is usually a paraphrase of that sentence.
Inference
Must be LOGICALLY supported by text. Not a wild guess — a small logical step from what's stated.
Vocab in Context
Replace the word with each answer choice. Which one makes the sentence make sense?
